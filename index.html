Early-Career Transition & Workplace Wellbeing Questionnaire

This questionnaire explores the challenges, experiences, and support needs of students, recent graduates, and early-career professionals during the transition from education to employment. It focuses on areas including job-search pressure, workplace connectedness, AI-related pressure, cross-cultural adjustment, and employer support.

Purpose Explore the challenges, experiences, and support needs of students, recent graduates, and early-career professionals during the transition from education to employment.
Methods note Captures participant perceptions and should be read separately from employer-practice mapping, which remains a desk-based research activity.
Participation Participation is voluntary, and responses will be treated anonymously. Questions can be skipped except where needed for consent or routing.
Ethics note The questionnaire does not collect clinical or diagnostic information and should not be understood as a psychological assessment.

Sensitive topics and support

Some questions in this questionnaire relate to workplace wellbeing, job-search pressure, confidence, career uncertainty, cross-cultural adjustment, and AI-related pressure. These topics may prompt reflection on stressful or uncomfortable experiences.

You may skip any non-required question or stop completing the questionnaire at any time before submission. If any topic causes concern or discomfort, further information about appropriate wellbeing and support services is provided in the Participant Information and Consent Form.

Samaritans: Call 116 123 free of charge, 24 hours a day, for confidential emotional support and someone to listen.

Mind Support Line: Call 0300 102 1234, Monday to Friday, 9am–6pm (except bank holidays), for emotional support and help finding appropriate services. This is not an emergency or crisis service.

This questionnaire is part of an Applied Extended Project at the University of Bristol. It explores the experiences, challenges, and support needs of students, recent graduates, and early-career professionals during the transition from education to employment. The questionnaire focuses on job-search pressure, workplace connectedness, AI-related pressure, cross-cultural adjustment, and employer support.

Responses will be used for academic research purposes only. Participation is voluntary, and responses will be treated anonymously. Participants may skip any question they do not wish to answer, except where a response is needed for consent or routing. The questionnaire does not collect clinical or diagnostic information and should not be understood as a psychological assessment.
